Traveling the World Basics for 2021

By Michelle Waldron

Step 1: Use Technology

Use the internet to discover where you might want to travel.

Consider using Google, Expedia, CDC, and country specific travel and tourism websites.

Check out the travel restrictions for each country you may want to visit.

Review exchange rates if you are traveling on a budget.

Step 2: Create a List
    Explore the world and create a list of countries and cities you want to visit.
    Consider if you can meet the restrictions and narrow your list based on this criteria.
    Also, be sure to keep your options open as health and safety information is changing regularly around the globe

Step 3: Travel Considerations

There are a variety of questions to consider when traveling in 2021.

  1. Do I want to travel to someplace populated or remote?
  2. What kind of activites do I want to particpate in?
  3. what type of activites are safe?
  4. Do I feel comfortable with indoor activities?
  5. Do I feel comfortable using public transportation?
  6. Should I stay at a hotel or someplace more private?
  7. What type of cancellation policy exists or can I take out travel insurance?
  8. Am I vaccinated?
  9. Does my insurance cover healthcare abroad?

These questions are just to get you thinking about how to plan, but more importantly the risks associated with travel.

Step 4: Make Reservations

How far in advance should you book airfare?

  • Typically air travel is most affordable 3 - 6 months before you intend to travel.

    • What reservations should you make in advance?

  • Lodging (hotel, AirBnB, etc.)
  • Rental cars
  • Tours, dining reservations
  • Transit or tourist pass

    Step 5: Fly Away

    Once all your important reservations are booked and you have reviewed local guidlines for health safety.

    1. Consider learning more about the local culture and customs of your destination.
    2. Research unique experiences and local foods.
    3. Most importantly, go off the beaten path. Get to know the locals, try to learn a few key words in their native language
    4. Test your language skills with sites like Babbel.

    Don't stress. Travel is not meant to cause anxiety or panic. It is an opportunity to learn by exploring outside your comfort zone.

    Have fun and safe travels!
